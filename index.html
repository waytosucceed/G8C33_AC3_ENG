Tenses : Present Continuous and Past Continuous Tense


The present continuous tense (also known as the present progressive tense) is used to describe actions that are happening right now or actions that are ongoing and in progress at the present moment.

Structure:
Positive/Affirmative sentence: Subject + am/is/are + verb + "ing"
Example: I am studying right now.

The past continuous tense (also known as the past progressive tense) is used to describe actions that were ongoing or in progress at a specific point in the past.
Structure:
Positive sentence: Subject + was/were + verb + "ing"
Example: They were playing soccer yesterday afternoon.

Words that I Learnt from the Story

Fleeting: Something that is brief, temporary, or lasting for only a short period of time.
Example: The joy of winning the game was fleeting, as the team quickly realized they had another tough match ahead.

Craftsmanship: The skill and quality of design and work shown in something made by hand; a high level of expertise in creating something with care and precision.
Example: The intricate details on the wooden sculpture reveal the artist's exceptional craftsmanship.

Perseverance: The quality of continuing to try or persist in doing something despite difficulties, obstacles, or delays in achieving success.
Example: Her perseverance through years of setbacks finally paid off when she completed her marathon race.
